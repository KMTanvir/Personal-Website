Research Focus

Core themes of my work

Clinical Trials Methodology

Design and analysis of cluster randomised and crossover trials, with a focus on statistical power and real world implementation.

Machine Learning for Health Systems

Applying machine learning to routine health data to address questions in epidemiology, health services, and implementation research.

Maternal and Newborn Health

Research on PCOS, pregnancy complications, delivery related risk factors, and care for preterm and low birthweight newborns.

Funded projects

Research grants

Principal Investigator

FertiMeter: a free of cost alternative to expensive diagnosis of Polycystic Ovarian Syndrome

A PI led project developing and evaluating an accessible, low cost screening approach for PCOS, aimed at expanding diagnostic reach in low resource settings.

Donor: Department of Foreign Affairs, Trade and Development, Canada (Global Affairs Canada)
Co-Principal Investigator

Awareness Assessment of Polycystic Ovary Syndrome among early reproductive aged women in Bangladesh

A Co-PI role on a nationally supported study raising awareness of PCOS to improve pregnancy outcomes, linking health systems research with maternal health.

Donor: University Grants Commission (UGC), Bangladesh
Methods and tools

How I work

Study design

Cluster randomised crossover trials, observational studies, case control studies, and meta analysis.

Modelling

Generalised linear models, longitudinal and mixed models, survival analysis, and machine learning for prediction.

Computing

R, Stata, Python, reproducible pipelines, and modern data visualisation.
