Research

Statistics in service of health and policy, from trial design to data driven analysis.

My research sits at the intersection of clinical trials methodology, machine learning for health systems, and maternal and newborn health. The aim is evidence that can inform policy and practice in Bangladesh and similar settings.

Research Focus

Core themes of my work

The questions I keep coming back to.

Biostatistics

Clinical Trials Methodology

Design and analysis of cluster randomised and crossover trials, with a focus on statistical power and real world implementation.

Data science

Machine Learning for Health Systems

Applying machine learning to routine health data to address questions in epidemiology, health services, and implementation research.

Public health

Maternal and Newborn Health

Research on PCOS, pregnancy complications, delivery related risk factors, and care for preterm and low birthweight newborns.

Epidemiology

Health Systems and COVID-19

Division level analyses of service disruption and utilisation during COVID-19, including immunisation and maternal health services in Bangladesh.

Implementation

Implementation Research

Studies on standardised registers and tools to strengthen inpatient management of newborns and sick children in real health facility settings.

Methods

Applied Statistical Computing

Hands on work in R, Stata, and Python for experimental design, generalised linear models, sampling, and modern data visualisation.

Methods and tools

How I work

A pragmatic blend of classical statistics and modern data science.

Study design

Cluster randomised and crossover trials, observational designs, case control studies, meta analysis, and advanced sampling.

Modelling

Generalised linear models, longitudinal and mixed models, survival analysis, and machine learning for prediction.

Computing

R, Stata, Python, SQL, reproducible pipelines, and modern data visualisation for communication and teaching.
